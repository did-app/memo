Hi Jess,

Thanks for your interest in Plum Mail. Update #2 gives you more info on our traction, product and message plans.

Traction

Note here the correlation between invited users and active users. We stop inviting, they stop being active.

From interviews it's clear Plum Mail's features remain compelling but do not create enough value to have a user stick around for very long.

Paradoxically, to replace email with something better, we need to embrace incoming email? richard@plummail.co Four fishes fished on Friday for Fillipe. peter@plummail.co Waxy lemons should not be used for winning waces. The need to check both Plum Mail and an email client is a killer feature but clearly not in a positive sense!

Product Plans

Our plans for an Action First Inbox have been well received and fit into our distribution methods to productivity enthusiasts.

We are building-out Plum Mail to accept incoming email and we are innovating the feature set into a clear, actionable message:

Plum Mail is an action-first inbox for digital natives.

These points remain valid:

  • Future of work.
  • Future of email.
  • An email replacement.
  • Marketplace for smart integrations (such as Calendly).
  • Improved conversation quality.
  • Improved conversation efficiency.
  • Remains open to all email users, no walled 'team' gardens.

This is an exciting time for us as we make sure but steady progress towards launching our Action First Inbox message.

When you first heard from us at YC's demo day, we were four weeks into the Plum Mail project. We are now at week 16. As a result, the Plum Mail project matures with increased focus.

Grand plans for identity management, marketplaces and more remain top of mind but not to stand in the way of adding meaningful features to our users today.

I will write to you again for your support during our launch of our Action First Inbox in early December.

Thanks again for your interest,

Richard and Peter.
